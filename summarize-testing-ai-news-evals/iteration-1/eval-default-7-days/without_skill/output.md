## Top Stories

### AI Is Reshaping Software Testing — But Not Without Concerns

The biggest theme this week is the dual-edged nature of AI in software development and testing.

**SmartBear Survey: 70% of Experts Worried About Quality** (March 18, Business Wire)
A new SmartBear survey finds that 70% of software professionals are concerned that application quality is suffering as AI-powered code generation accelerates development speed beyond what testing practices can keep up with. The tension between velocity and quality is a growing industry pressure point.

**Banks Warned: AI Writes Code Faster Than It Can Be Tested** (March 10, QA Financial)
Financial institutions are being flagged for compliance and risk exposure as AI development velocity outpaces their testing programs. Regulators and internal risk teams are taking note.

**Why Your AI Is Failing in Production** (March 18, Security Boulevard)
A piece examining the gap between AI system behavior in testing and production environments, arguing that strategic QA practices need to evolve to address non-deterministic AI outputs.

### Methodology and Practice

**From Chaos Engineering to Resilience Testing** (March 19, dqindia.com)
The industry is moving beyond traditional test-break-fix cycles. This piece covers how resilience testing and chaos engineering are becoming mainstream QA disciplines, especially for distributed and cloud-native systems.

**Playwright-Native Developer Experience** (March 19, Applitools Blog)
Applitools published a technical post on managing visual differences in Playwright testing using a single configuration flag to choose between three different diff strategies. Practical and tool-specific, but worth a read if you're working with Playwright.

**BDD in Practice: Where Given/When/Then Actually Helps** (March 21, dev.to)
A grounded take on Behavior-Driven Development — the author argues BDD is genuinely valuable for business-critical acceptance criteria when non-technical stakeholders are actively involved, but warns against applying it universally across all test types.

**The 5 Things I Check Before Marking Agent Code Verified** (March 21, dev.to)
With AI agents increasingly writing and modifying code, this checklist-style post covers what to verify before signing off on agentic deployments: autonomy levels, logic error cascades, privilege escalation, reasoning transparency, and post-deployment monitoring.

Prince Kohli, CEO of Sauce Labs, made a provocative claim: "the $1 trillion software quality sector has been developing incorrectly for two decades." He's calling for fundamental changes in how the industry approaches quality — not just tooling improvements.

**91% of Software Companies Using AI to Cut Costs** (March 17, GlobeNewswire)
A Goodfirms survey finds near-universal adoption of AI for cost reduction in software development. Testing's role in this cost-cutting picture is a key subtext.

**UK's FCA Tightens Incident Reporting for Financial Software** (March 20, QA Financial)
The UK Financial Conduct Authority is strengthening third-party testing and incident reporting requirements. Regulated industries are facing increasing compliance pressure around software quality practices.

## Key Themes This Week

1. **AI velocity vs. quality**: The dominant tension — AI is generating code faster than teams can test it. Industry surveys, analyst pieces, and CEO commentary all circling this.
2. **Agentic testing platforms**: Tricentis and others are moving toward autonomous, agent-driven testing infrastructure as a response to AI-generated codebases.
3. **Regulatory pressure**: Financial regulators (FCA, and ongoing banking sector scrutiny) are increasing requirements around software quality and testing documentation.
4. **Practitioner tools**: Playwright continues to dominate browser automation discussion; BDD adoption patterns and AI agent verification checklists reflect the day-to-day reality of testers in 2026.
