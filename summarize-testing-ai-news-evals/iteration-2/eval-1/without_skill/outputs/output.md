**Date: March 28, 2026**

## 1. AI and Agentic Testing Dominate the Industry

The biggest trend across the software testing landscape right now is the rapid shift toward agentic AI — AI systems that autonomously explore applications, generate tests, and evaluate results with minimal human intervention.

- **SmartBear launched BearQ** (March 18, 2026), an agentic QA system that autonomously performs quality assurance tasks. This was accompanied by a SmartBear survey finding that **70% of software experts are concerned that application quality is suffering** as AI-accelerated code development outpaces testing practices.

- **Tricentis launched an Agentic Quality Engineering Platform** (March 13, 2026), positioning itself to serve AI-driven software development pipelines. The company is expanding globally and bolstering its leadership team.

- **UiPath's Test Cloud** is advancing a three-phase AI testing roadmap: AI-assisted individual steps ("Autopilot for Testers"), customer-built custom agents, and full multi-agent orchestration. UiPath's GM noted that while 70–80% of developers use AI coding tools, QA adoption remains below 50%.

## 3. Standards and Global Initiatives

- **ETRI (South Korea) established ISO/IEC TS 42119-2** (announced March 25, 2026), a historic first in international AI testing standards. The standard defines procedures for verifying AI system safety and reliability across the full lifecycle — covering data quality, model performance, bias assessment, adversarial testing, and drift testing. It introduces "risk-based testing" for proactive AI risk identification. Future work includes standards for Red Teaming and Generative AI testing.
